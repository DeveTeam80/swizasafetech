Types of Castors



There are various types of castors, each designed for specific applications. Below are some of the most common types:

1. Swivel Castors

  • These allow the wheel to rotate 360 degrees, offering excellent maneuverability.
  • Ideal for environments where quick and flexible movement is needed.

2. Fixed Castors

  • Fixed in one direction and do not swivel.
  • Best for straight-line movements and stability.

3. Brake Castors

  • Equipped with a braking mechanism to keep the trolley stationary when needed.
  • Perfect for safety-critical applications.

4. Heavy-Duty Castors

  • Built to carry substantial weights, making them ideal for industrial use.
  • Commonly found in factories and warehouses.

5. Twin-Wheel Castors

  • Designed with two wheels for better load distribution.
  • Provide enhanced stability and mobility.

Key Features of Castors

High-quality castors come with specific features that enhance their usability:

  • 1. Material Options: Made from rubber, polyurethane, stainless steel, or nylon, depending on the usage requirements.
  • 2. Load-Bearing Capacity: Castors are designed to carry varying weights, from light-duty to heavy-duty applications.
  • 3. Noise Reduction: Some castors are engineered for silent operation, perfect for environments like hospitals or libraries.
  • 4. Corrosion Resistance: Essential for outdoor or moisture-prone environments.
  • 5. Shock Absorption: Ideal for delicate equipment transportation.

Applications of Castors in Trolleys

Castors find widespread use in a variety of sectors, especially when paired with trolleys. Here are some notable applications:

1. Industrial Use

  • In factories and warehouses, castors make it easy to move heavy machinery, raw materials, and finished goods.
  • Heavy-duty castors ensure that industrial trolleys can handle significant loads without compromising stability.

2. Healthcare

  • Hospitals and clinics use trolleys fitted with swivel or brake castors for transporting medical equipment, supplies, and even patients.
  • Noise-reducing castors are essential for maintaining a quiet environment.

3. Retail and Shopping

  • Shopping carts and retail trolleys rely on durable castors for smooth operation in high-traffic areas.
  • Swiza Safetech LLP supplies castors that ensure longevity even with constant use.

4. Retail and Shopping

  • Hotels and restaurants use castor-fitted carts for room service, cleaning, and luggage transportation.

5. Domestic Use

  • Trolleys for household chores, gardening, or DIY projects use light-duty castors for convenience.

6. Logistics and Transportation

  • Castors are vital for moving goods in loading docks, airports, and shipping facilities.

How to Identify the Right Castor for Your Trolley?

Choosing the appropriate castor requires careful consideration of the following factors:

1. Load Capacity

  • Assess the total weight the trolley will carry. Overloading castors can lead to damage or accidents.

2. Type of Surface

  • For smooth indoor surfaces, rubber or polyurethane castors work best.
  • For outdoor or uneven terrains, pneumatic castors are more suitable.

3. Mobility Requirements

  • Use swivel castors for better maneuverability and fixed castors for straight-line stability.

4. Environment

  • For corrosive or high-moisture areas, stainless steel or specially coated castors are necessary.

5. Temperature Resistance

  • Ensure the castors can withstand extreme temperatures if used in cold storage or hot environments.

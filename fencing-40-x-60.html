Without strong and reliable fencing solutions, industrial machine safety is just impossible. Swiza Safetech, being the leading Machine Guarding Manufacturer in India and a recognized Safety Fencing Supplier in Pune, brings one such state-of-the-art solution in the form of its Fencing 40 x 60 Column Series. It is an advanced fencing system, robust, versatile, and protective. Major usages for it would be within mesh panel applications, where the mesh would play its role in safeguarding machinery while simultaneously providing safety to personnel who are at a close distance from these machines. The 40 x 60 Column Series increases the strength and stability in any application.

Product Description


The Swiza Fencing 40 x 60 Column Series is an advanced safety solution designed to manage modern industrial environments' demanding requirements. This fencing system is engineered to provide superior protection and support, making it an ideal choice where rigidity and resistance to external forces may be required. These enhancements make the 40 x 60 Column Series especially appropriate for any industry where machinery safety is of the greatest importance, such as manufacturing plants, automated warehouses, and other high-risk environments.

Key Features and Benefits


The Swiza 40 x 60 Column Series has a few important features that help it stand out among other fencing solutions in the market:
More Strength and Stability: It is in the increased size of its columns that lies the most notable improvement of the 40 x 60 Column Series. The 40 x 60 columns give a more solid support. Added strength ensures this fencing remains intact and functional, even in environments with heavy machinery in use or where the fencing might be under considerable stress.
Versatile Design: The most remarkable feature of the Swiza 40 x 60 Column Series is its versatile design. The system is engineered to support a variety of mesh panel configurations so that businesses can generate the type of fencing that most effectively satisfies their particular safety concerns. This flexibility will allow the 40 x 60 Column Series to work in applications as diverse as safeguarding robotic arms in manufacturing plants to secure perimeters for automated material-handling systems in warehouses.
Improved Durability: The durability of the 40 x 60 Column Series is one of the most significant design factors. Thus, the columns are made of quality raw materials to improve their resistance to corrosion, wear, and any other tear, hence offering a long service life with minimal maintenance. This means that in the long run, businesses have less cost due to reduced instances of replacement or even repair. The fencing system is designed for the rigors demanded in industrial environments for the effects of chemicals, temperature changes, and physical impacts.
Installation Ease: For all its sturdiness, the 40 x 60 Column Series is still designed for easy installation. Its modular design allows on-site assembly to be quick and easy to set up with minimal tools and equipment. Easy setup can reduce setup time and minimize lost productivity. Added ease of use within the system allows for easier modification or expansion of the fencing as required, therefore allowing even greater flexibility when growing an operation.

Uses and Applications


The 40 x 60 Column Series is an integral safety component within the industrial setting, protecting both machines and automated systems. It is typically erected around the periphery of machines, physically closing them off to intrusion, while providing easy visibility into the surrounding area. This transparency is important in applications where it is necessary to view the operation of machinery for safety or efficiency purposes.
Here's how the 40 x 60 Column Series is typically used:
Site Preparation: Proper preparation of the site will be required before the installation of any fencing system. The area where the fencing is going to be installed shall be free of litter, parts of machinery, or other obstructions which might hinder the installation process. Ensuring a clean and level surface will go a long way in providing a secure and stable installation.
Setting up Column: Once the site is prepared column marks are made where 40 x 60 columns shall be placed. The spacing shall be worked out after considering the mesh panel specifications; after measuring the locations, the columns need to securely hold on to the floor with anchor fasteners grouting for a strong base for the rest of the fencing system.
Mesh Panel Installation: After the columns are in place, the fixing of the mesh panels follows. These panels should be attached to the columns securely and without gaps between them and the panels. The integrity of these barriers for safety relies on this step; even small gaps can destroy the effectiveness of the fencing system.
The 40 x 60 Column Series caters to a wide array of industries needing both safety and durability. The more common uses include manufacturing plants, where the fencing safeguards workers from dangers associated with automated machinery and robotic arms; automated warehouses, where material handling systems require a protection zone created around them; and assembly lines that prevent the accidental contact of moving parts.

Comparison of 40 x 40 Column Series and 40 x 60 Column Series


Although the 40 x 40 and 40 x 60 Column Series are both robust, efficient solutions that cover most safety needs, there are a few differences which have some bearing on the choice:
Column Dimensions: Probably the most visible aspect of difference between the two series has to do with the column dimensions. The 40 × 60 columns are larger and more resistant to stress and impact, compared to the 40 × 40, which makes them suitable for heavy-duty applications where the run of fencing can be under a lot of stress or impact.
Load-Bearing Capacity: With the increased size of the 40 x 60 columns, so too is their capacity for load-bearing increased. These columns are less likely to bend or buckle under pressure and will maintain their integrity in areas where extra weight may be expected to be held from the fencing, such as heavy machinery or where the fencing may be used to hang equipment or tools of some sort.
Installation Flexibility: While the 40 × 60 columns have greater strength, the 40 × 40 columns are also strong & are more manageable and easier to handle due to their reduced size, making them more suitable for installations with reduced space or when the fencing system is frequently to be relocated or rearranged. On the other side, the 40 × 60 columns give more flexibility regarding the type of mesh panels that can be mounted to them, offering more personalized solutions.
Swiza Safetech's Fencing 40 x 60 Column Series is the real catch for customers in search of enhanced strength, durability, and versatility in their safety systems. Upgrading or implementing new safety measures, Swiza Safetech fencing solutions ensure protection to keep operations flowing smoothly and safely, whereby equipment and personnel will be fully protected in all the harshest industrial environments.

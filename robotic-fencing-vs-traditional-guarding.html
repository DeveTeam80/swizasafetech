As automation continues to revolutionize manufacturing and industrial processes, ensuring the safety of human workers while robots perform their tasks is paramount. This is where robotic fencing and traditional guarding come into play. Both methods aim to create a safe working environment for humans and robots to coexist, but they differ in their approach.



This blog post delves into a detailed comparison of robotic fencing and traditional guarding, helping you choose the most suitable solution for your specific needs. We'll explore the functionalities, advantages, limitations, and key considerations for each method to guide you in making an informed decision


Understanding Robotic Fencing

Robotic fencing, also known as safety fencing or robot cages, utilizes a physical barrier to separate robots from the workspace where humans operate. This barrier is typically constructed from lightweight yet sturdy materials like mesh panels, aluminum profiles, or polycarbonate sheets. Robotic fencing systems often incorporate safety interlocks and sensors that trigger an emergency stop if a human enters the restricted area while the robot is active.


Here's a closer look at the functionalities of robotic fencing:

Physical Separation: Robotic fencing creates a distinct demarcation between the robotic work zone and the human work area, preventing accidental contact
Safety Interlocks: These electronic locks are integrated with the fencing and the robot's control system. If the fencing is breached, the interlock automatically stops the robot's operation, preventing injuries
Visual Cues: The fencing itself serves as a visual reminder to personnel that they are entering a zone with potential robotic activity.
Flexible Design: Robotic fencing systems can be customized in size and configuration to accommodate different robot sizes, workspaces, and layouts.


Exploring Traditional Guarding

Traditional guarding encompasses various safety measures employed to protect personnel from machinery and moving parts. These can include:
Fixed Guards: Permanently attached barriers made from metal, plastic, or other materials to shield hazardous areas
Interlocking Guards: Similar to fixed guards, but with a hinged or sliding mechanism that requires deliberate activation to access the hazardous area, often triggering a temporary shut-off of the machinery.
Light Curtains: Utilize beams of light to detect an obstruction within the guarded zone. When a beam is broken, the machinery automatically stops
Safety Gates: Swinging or sliding gates that control access to hazardous areas

Key Considerations for Choosing the Right Solution

The choice between robotic fencing and traditional guarding depends on several factors specific to your application:


Robot Size and Movement: For large robots or operations with unpredictable movements, robotic fencing provides better overall safety coverage
Workspace Layout: If space is a constraint, traditional guarding might be a more viable option. However, for complex workspaces, robotic fencing can offer greater flexibility in design
Risk Assessment: Conduct a thorough risk assessment to identify potential hazards associated with your robotic operations. This will help determine the level of protection required.
Budget: While traditional guarding is generally less expensive, consider the long-term costs. Robotic fencing might be a worthwhile investment for high-risk applications or industries with stricter safety regulations.
Maintainability: Ensure chosen methods allow for easy access for maintenance and repair of the robot without compromising safety protocols



Additional Factors to Consider
Regulations: Some industries might have specific regulations regarding safety measures for robotic operations. Ensure your chosen solution complies with any relevant regulations
Future Expansion: If you anticipate expanding your robotic capabilities in the future, consider the scalability and flexibility of the safety solution.
Employee Training: Regardless of the chosen method, providing comprehensive safety training for your workforce is crucial.
